Being part of [Laravel](https://laravel.com) ecosystem has created a habit of doing things quickly; without any pain. [Filament](https://filamentphp.com) has earned its place at the top within other tools/packages. Built by [Dan Harrin](https://www.linkedin.com/in/danharrin), Filament has earned its place as the go-to package for building modern admin panels in Laravel.

But while Filament gives you tools for managing your resources, content, and users, one thing was still missing: log visibility. And digging the `laravel.log` file is not fun.

So I built [**Filament Log Viewer**](https://github.com/achyutkneupane/filament-log-viewer), a plugin that lets you browse, search, and filter your Laravel logs right from the Filament UI.
